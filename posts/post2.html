I Killed My Spotify Subscription (And Why You Should Too)

03/05/2026

I am fucking tired of subscriptions.

Open your bank account and look at your transactions for the month. $10 here, $15 there. They charge you to listen to music, they charge you to watch shows, they charge you for cloud space, and soon enough, they’ll charge you to breathe. We’ve been sold the false illusion of convenience in exchange for emptying our pockets month after month, all to own absolutely nothing.

"When you actually sit down and do the math, you realize you're bleeding over $80 a year just for the permission to listen to music. Eighty bucks annually to rent files you will never own."

And for what? Just so they can package your data into a shiny year-end review? I’m also sick of paying premium prices just to get a summary of my top tracks and total listening hours, all so I can post it on my Instagram stories—as if anyone on the internet actually gives a shit about how many hundreds of hours you spent blasting "LA QUEMONA" on repeat. It's ridiculous.

If a corporation decides to wipe out your favorite album tomorrow due to a stupid licensing issue, you just have to sit there and take it. Enough is enough.

That’s why I decided to tell Spotify to go to hell and built my own web platform. I named it Sovereign.

Why Sovereign? Because sovereignty is about absolute power, self-governance, and independence. It means nobody else has authority over what is yours. In a world where Big Tech dictates how, when, and at what price you can access your own digital culture, building this app was my way of reclaiming total sovereignty over my data, my files, and my hardware.

I don’t want another bloated app eating up my machine's RAM, nor interfaces cluttered with garbage, nor algorithms pushing trash music I don't care about. I wanted something minimalist, fast, something that runs perfectly in the background on any phone or computer, and that operates strictly under my own rules.

So, I dusted off an old computer I had lying around the house collecting dust, turned it into my own server, and deployed my code there. Now, I can access my music from anywhere in the world with an internet connection, without paying a single cent to anyone. I am the sole ruler of my library.

This Is Not a Business

I want to make one thing fucking clear: I don’t want your money. I have zero interest in putting ads on this, creating a "Premium" plan, or selling your listening data to some marketing agency. I didn't build this to get rich or to beg investors for cash.

The project is already up on GitHub, and it is 100% open-source.

The idea is that if you are also sick of paying to breathe, you stop complaining, go check out the repository, clone the code, and spin up your own instance of Sovereign on whatever toaster of a computer you have at home. It’s time to take back control of your wallet and declare your own digital independence.

The code is out there, ready and functional for anyone who wants to stop throwing money at corporations. Go take a look at the repo, set it up, and dump your subscriptions.

It’s time to stop renting our culture and start hosting it ourselves.
